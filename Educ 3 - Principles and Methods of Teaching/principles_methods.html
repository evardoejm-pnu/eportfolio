View Submitted Document
Critical Analysis of Classroom Observation: Bread and Pastry Production
Reflection:

The most valuable takeaway was the realization that extrinsic motivation is a fundamental, sometimes necessary, driver of engagement in the primary and secondary levels. Observing the teacher creatively use pop culture references—like framing the TLE lesson as "PanDaria: The Enchanted Academy of Baking and Delight"—showed me that connecting a lesson to relatable themes can powerfully instill interest in students who might lack intrinsic motivation. This directly challenges my past collegiate belief that academic interest should be self-driven, leading to a professional commitment to actively seek out and implement strategies that make content appealing and relevant.

Finalized Detailed Lesson Plan: Integrating 21st Century Skills and UN SDGs in Asian Historical Analysis
Reflection:

The final preparation of this lesson plan was the most crucial step in solidifying my confidence, demonstrating a deep and coherent idea that teaching at the secondary level requires holistic integration. The biggest shift from the draft (Entry 7) was moving the SDGs from a mere mention in the margin to the central pedagogical engine of the lesson. This commitment to designing learning experiences that prioritize global citizenship proves a professional evolution that will have a lasting impact on how I approach every future lesson.

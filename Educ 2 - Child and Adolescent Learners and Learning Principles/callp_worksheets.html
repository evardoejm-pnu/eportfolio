2E-Educ-2 | The Child and Adolescent Learners and Learning Principles

This section compiles three mandatory worksheets submitted for the course, each demonstrating key concepts about student development and learning principles. Each artifact is paired with a reflection on the personal growth gained from the exercise.

Diagramming the Principles of Development and Learning

Date Submitted: September 3, 2025

This visual worksheet required creating two distinct diagrams to illustrate key concepts from the course readings. The first uses the "Jeep Pasada Principles" metaphor to explain concepts like Cephalo-Caudal and Individual Differences. The second uses a "Vine" metaphor to map cognitive principles, connecting them to the progressive stages of mastery. This exercise was essential for physically translating dense theoretical concepts into usable visual models.

Diagramming the Principles of Development and Learning
Reflection:

This worksheet really helped me grasp the inter-relationship between complex principles, especially between Cephalo-Caudal (head-to-toe development) and the Spiral Nature of Development. The "Vine" diagram was particularly effective in visualizing how learning is scaffolded and why concepts like motivation and self-regulation are crucial for reaching true mastery (the "flowering vine" stage). It highlighted the shift from teacher-led practice to learner autonomy, which is a massive takeaway for me.

Personal Professional Development Plan

Date Submitted: September 22, 2025

This comprehensive professional development plan served as a strategic self-assessment, requiring me to identify my current strengths and weaknesses in teaching and content knowledge (e.g., my extensive statistics teaching experience vs. my foundational mathematical weaknesses). The plan outlines specific, actionable steps and timelines to achieve my career goals of becoming both a data scientist and a statistics professor.

View Submitted Documents
Personal Professional Development Plan
Reflection:

The biggest realization from this exercise was that teaching is not a career destination but a platform for continuous growth. By systematically mapping my career aspirations (data science and professorship), I understood that my primary weakness—foundational math—is actually the most crucial area for plasticity and investment. The plan transformed my abstract aspirations into a series of measurable, time-bound objectives, reinforcing the idea that self-regulation is key to professional growth. It was like giving myself a detailed, 5-year roadmap.

Classroom Observation Tool: Fostering Engagement and Delight

Date Submitted: October 19, 2025
Context of Observation:

As part of our course requirements, we were asked to observe classes in FGEEL-ITL. I chose to observe Dr. Lorena A. Castro's TLE class on October 10, 2025, as it aligned with my current teaching assignment (Grade 7 TLE classes) and offered a convenient schedule. The demonstration was conducted by two student teachers, Sir Marl Fortaleza and Ms. Kwen Faelnar.

This final worksheet was a classroom observation summary I conducted on a peer's teaching demonstration (Ms. Kwen Faelnar's TLE lesson on Pan de Coco). The task required me to use the PNU-approved observation tool to assess Focus on the Learner, the Learning Process, and the Learning Environment. Analyzing how my peer used high-level engagement (Encantadia theme, video demos) and immediate inquiry-based questions helped me solidify my understanding of blending pedagogy with affective learning.

View Submitted Document
Classroom Observation Tool: Fostering Engagement and Delight
Reflection:

Observing a peer was super helpful because it made all those theoretical concepts feel real. Ms. Kwen nailed the affective part of teaching—using the Encantadia theme to teach baking was genius! It made the students excited and ready to learn. I realized that a perfect lesson plan isn't just about covering content; it's about creating delight and engagement so powerful that the content sticks. The biggest lesson was seeing how she immediately used an inquiry method ("What Ifs?") to push them from knowing how to bake to understanding the science behind it. It confirmed that blending fun with critical thinking is the ultimate goal.
